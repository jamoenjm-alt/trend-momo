Read before you trust it

1. Deeper drawdown than the index. Waterflow fell ~35% in 2022 vs the S&P's ~24%. Higher return came with more pain, not less.

2. The megacap premium may be a regime artifact. This 2010–2026 window was a historic mega-cap/tech run. In 2000–2010 the giants lagged the index. This test cannot see that risk — it isn't in the data.

3. It's concentrated. 10 names, all US large-cap, heavily tech. No diversification benefit; if megacaps roll over together, so does this.

Point-in-time top-10 per year (no NVDA/TSLA hindsight), 15bps costs, no parameters tuned after seeing results. Honest, but not a promise.
